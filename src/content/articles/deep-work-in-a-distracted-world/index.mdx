## What is Deep Work?

Deep work is a concept popularized by Cal Newport. Itâ€™s **the ability to focus without distraction on a cognitively demanding task**.  

Characteristics:

- **High concentration:** No multitasking or interruptions.  
- **High value output:** Produces meaningful results.  
- **Difficult to replicate:** Hard for others to automate or outsource.

Contrast this with **shallow work**: emails, meetings, and social media scrolling, which are low-value, fragmented tasks.

## Making Deep Work a Habit

- **Start small:** Begin with 1 session/day, gradually increase.  
- **Track your output:** Measure outcomes, not hours spent.  
- **Reflect weekly:** Identify what disrupted focus and adjust your environment or schedule.  

- **Eliminate shallow work when possible:** Delegate, automate, or batch routine tasks.

## Overcoming Common Challenges

- **Interruptions:** Communicate availability to teammates; use visible signals (e.g., headphones).  
- **Mental fatigue:** Take breaks, hydrate, and sleep well.  
- **Consistency:** Ritualize sessions; consistency beats intensity.
